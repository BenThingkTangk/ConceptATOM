Scene 1 · Advisor experience

One Trusted Advisor. One hour. Two timelines.

This is the moment Pathfinder either feels like a form… or like a strategy room. The only difference is whether ATOM is in the loop.

Today’s storyline

1. Advisor opens Pathfinder and walks through a 60+ minute IQA.

2. The submission vanishes into a 3–5 day engineering queue.

3. Someone spends 2–4 hours researching providers and rebuilding a matrix.

4. A static PDF and recommendation appear in the advisor’s inbox.

5. The advisor tries to re‑ignite the customer conversation days later.

ATOM’s storyline

1. As the IQA starts, ATOM quietly begins listening and structuring the session.

2. A side panel suggests next questions, phrasing, and objection handling.

3. Dynamic Matrices fill in and re‑rank in real time as answers land.

4. By minute 25, a ranked recommendation and story arc are drafted.

5. The call ends with a clear path and a customer‑ready artifact, not a backlog.

Scene 2 · Dynamic Matrices

The matrix becomes the engine, not the output.

Dynamic Matrices are already the centerpiece of Pathfinder. ATOM turns them into a real‑time decision engine that everyone can see and trust.

Capability Today With ATOM
How it’s built Manually, after the call Auto‑populated during the call from IQA answers and signals
Ranking providers Static per submission Re‑ranks in real time when needs change
Explaining scores Depends on who has time to write Every score has a “why” and a source behind it
Deliverables One‑off docs built by hand Advisor and customer versions published automatically
Auto‑population

As IQA answers land, providers appear, scores are assigned, and mismatches fall away automatically. Advisors never rebuild matrices row by row.

Live re‑ranking

New compliance requirement, budget guardrail, deployment preference – mention it, and the matrix reshuffles so “top right” always matches the real ask.

Explainable by design

Click into any cell and see requirements, provider facts, and documentation. No “because the AI told me so.”

Dual‑mode outputs

Internal view with confidence and coaching hints; external view that makes the recommendation story obvious and easy to forward.

You can feel this pattern live in Antimatter’s AI Vendor Matrix – a Dynamic Matrix for enterprise AI platforms.

Scene 3 · Atlas & IntenIQ

Global data center decisions with a signal‑aware co‑pilot.

Atlas gives Pathfinder a map of the world. IntenIQ gives it a sense of intent. ATOM turns both into live guidance for the advisor.

Atlas as the map

Atlas knows who does what, and where, around the globe. ATOM turns IQA answers into precise Atlas queries – no filter gymnastics mid‑call.

IntenIQ as the signal layer

IntenIQ tracks intent – urgency, risk tolerance, appetite for change – and lets those signals nudge rankings and talking points.

“Ask Atlas” as the voice

Instead of “let me get back to you,” advisors ask Atlas questions in natural language and get structured answers they can read out and show on screen.

Integrated into the matrix

Atlas results and IntenIQ signals feed straight into Dynamic Matrices, so the “table” advisors see is grounded in live provider data and real intent.

Scene 4 · Architecture

Pathfinder stays the system of record. ATOM becomes the brain.

Pathfinder owns workflow and data. ATOM plugs in as an evidence‑first, session‑centric intelligence layer. No rip‑and‑replace required.

Pathfinder

Handles login, IQA configuration, submissions, routing, and UI – the front door and canonical record for every session.

ATOM services

Listen to IQAs and calls, normalize them, retrieve provider facts, score fit, coach the advisor, and assemble artifacts in the background.

Knowledge & providers

Provider profiles, Dynamic Matrices, Atlas data, case studies, and compliance details live as a governed knowledge graph – ATOM’s ground truth.

Security & governance

Encryption, roles, audit, and redaction follow familiar enterprise patterns. ATOM fits into Avant’s controls, rather than creating a new island.

This is built on Antimatter’s existing ATOM Agentic Platform – an end‑to‑end agentic stack already running in other domains.
